Summer 2024 - Dragon Build Reliability

It was the morning of the fall career fair, and the technical clubs were working on creating their tabling presences for the event. I requisitoned the help of public safety to assist in carting my club's materials down the hill from the academic buildings to the gymnasium. I set up my table. My flats tore up my heels.

Katie McManus approached my display. "Hi," she said. "Hi," I responded.

"Wanna work for SpaceX?" I think the conversation started vaguely like that. Suddenly I found myself interviewing for a position in Cape Canaveral for an as-of-yet-undecided launch related internship position. I had no idea what to expect, but I arrived to find I was working on Cape's build reliability team for the Dragon spacecraft. Whatever it was, coincidence, chance, luck-- this was an enormous moment of serpendipity, and boy am I greatful. I had too much fun.

A hilariously blurry photo of me at the VAB.

My internship this summer revolved primarily around 4 projects. The first implicated both structures and computer science. The second addressed RF shielding technology on-vehicle. The third was an automation for internal tracking and work tools. Finally, I worked on a project that involved the tools used to apply a heat-resistent material to the vehicle. Other assorted tasks included root-cause work on life support systems and a supportive role on a payload for an upcoming mission, fram2.

The major skill gains here from the technical perspective included advancements in my comfort with software development for end-users, a basic understanding of structures concepts which I did not have before, exposure to avionics systems and life support systems, and advanced my root-cause skills.
